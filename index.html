A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam rose from humble beginnings in Rameswaram to become a leading scientist and the 11th President of India. His pioneering work in India's space and missile programs, including the development of the SLV-III and the Agni and Prithvi missiles, significantly advanced the nation's defense capabilities. As President, he was beloved as the 'People's President,' inspiring millions with his vision for a developed India and his emphasis on education and innovation. His legacy continues to motivate future generations to dream big and work tirelessly for the betterment of society.

Biographies

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble family.
  • Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and pursued aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Started career at Aeronautical Development Establishment of DRDO.
  • Joined ISRO, becoming Project Director for India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • Contributions include successful deployment of Rohini satellite and development of ballistic missile technology, including Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Elected 11th President of India in 2002, served until 2007.
  • Known as 'People's President' for his accessibility and focus on youth empowerment and education.
  • Visionary leader, authored "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium," emphasizing education, technology, and sustainable development.
  • Legacy as an inspiration for millions worldwide, even after his passing on July 27, 2015.
